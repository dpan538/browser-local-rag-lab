# Claims And Non-Claims

This repository is intended to be reviewed as a controlled research artifact.
The following boundaries are part of the method, not after-the-fact caveats.

## Claims

- We claim controlled generation reliability under contract/gold evidence
  packets.
- We claim delivered-answer contract compliance for
  `v3.3_contract_top3_300_delivered`.
- We claim that deterministic refusal and source/rights lanes are appropriate
  for fields that should not be freely generated by a small model.
- We claim that postprocessed prose plus evidence-tag injection can reduce
  latency relative to prompt-only guardrails while preserving the delivered
  evidence contract.
- We claim measured latency on the recorded browser/WebGPU setup.
- We claim that the evidence-field contract layer remained stable in additional
  model/runtime probes from V4.1. Prose quality and latency remain
  model/runtime dependent.

## Non-Claims

- We do not claim full archive-scale retrieval recall.
- We do not claim that raw top-3/top-8 retrieval is product-ready.
- We do not claim raw Qwen semantic correctness without deterministic lanes,
  evidence-tag injection, and prose postprocessing.
- We do not claim generated prose is archive evidence.
- We do not claim hardware-general latency without replication on additional
  devices and browser versions.
- We do not claim that automated quality screens replace human semantic review.
- We do not claim that the 15-row robustness miniset is a comprehensive
  adversarial benchmark.
- We do not claim broad cross-model generalization from the V4.1 probes alone.

## Paper-Safe Summary

The safest paper claim is:

> We evaluate a contract-first architecture for browser-local small-model RAG
> in rights-aware digital archives. Under a controlled gold-evidence packet
> condition, deterministic answer lanes, evidence-tag injection, and
> postprocessed prose reduce latency and contract failures while preserving
> source/rights boundaries.
